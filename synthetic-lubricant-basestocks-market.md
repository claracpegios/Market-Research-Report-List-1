<p><p>Synthetic lubricant basestocks are categorized into several types based on their chemical composition and properties. Polyalkylene Glycols (PAGs) are known for their excellent thermal stability and water solubility. Polyalphaolefins (PAOs) provide superior low-temperature fluidity and high viscosity index. Polyisobutenes are valued for their flexibility and low volatility. Phosphate Esters offer fire-resistant characteristics and are often used in aviation applications. Synthetic Esters combine biodegradability with excellent lubrication properties. Other basestocks may include advanced formulations catering to specific performance requirements.</p></p>
