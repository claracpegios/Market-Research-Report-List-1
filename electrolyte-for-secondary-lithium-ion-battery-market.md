<p><p>The electrolyte in secondary lithium-ion batteries plays a crucial role in facilitating the movement of lithium ions between the anode and cathode. Liquid electrolytes, typically composed of lithium salts dissolved in organic solvents, offer high ionic conductivity and flexibility but can pose safety risks due to volatility. Solid electrolytes, on the other hand, provide enhanced safety and stability by eliminating flammability concerns, though they often face challenges in achieving high ionic conductivity and effective interfaces with electrodes. Both types are vital for advancing battery technology.</p></p>
