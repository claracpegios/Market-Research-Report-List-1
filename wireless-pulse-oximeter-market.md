<p><p>The wireless pulse oximeter market features two primary types: fingertip and wrist type. Fingertip pulse oximeters, typically compact and portable, provide quick and accurate oxygen saturation readings by clipping onto a finger, making them ideal for personal use and immediate tracking. In contrast, wrist-type pulse oximeters are designed for continuous monitoring, often featuring straps for comfortable wear and integration with other health tracking devices. Both types are popular for their convenience and non-invasive monitoring capabilities in various settings.</p></p>
