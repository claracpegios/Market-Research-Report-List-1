<p><p>The low-voltage ceramic capacitor market is divided into two main types: organic and inorganic ceramic capacitors. Organic ceramic capacitors utilize organic materials in their dielectric layers, offering advantages like flexibility and lightweight designs, making them suitable for consumer electronics. In contrast, inorganic ceramic capacitors employ inorganic dielectric materials, known for their stability and reliability under high voltage conditions. These capacitors are widely used in industrial applications and power electronics, offering superior performance in demanding environments.</p></p>
