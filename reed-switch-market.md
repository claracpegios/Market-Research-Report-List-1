<p><p>A reed switch is an electrically operated switch that consists of two ferromagnetic contacts sealed within a glass envelope. When exposed to a magnetic field, the contacts close or open, allowing the flow of electricity. <p><p>Reed switches are classified into different market types based on their internal configurations and functionalities. Form A switches are normally open, closing when a magnetic field is applied. Form B switches are normally closed, opening in the presence of a magnetic field. Form C switches are dual-function, offering both normally open and normally closed outputs. Other market types may include specialized or customized reed switches tailored for specific applications, such as sensors, relays, and electronic devices across various industries.</p></p>
