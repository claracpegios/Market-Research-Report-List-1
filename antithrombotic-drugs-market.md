<p><p>The antithrombotic drugs market includes several key agents that inhibit blood clotting, each with distinct mechanisms and applications. Apixaban, Rivaroxaban, and Edoxaban are direct oral anticoagulants that block factor Xa, preventing thrombus formation. Dabigatran inhibits thrombin directly. Fondaparinux also targets factor Xa but is a synthetic analog of heparin. Heparin, a traditional anticoagulant, is used for immediate effects in both prevention and treatment of thromboembolic disorders. Together, these drugs cater to diverse patient needs in managing coagulation disorders.</p></p>
